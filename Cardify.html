Project Overview & Contributions

With the pandemic beginning to truly cement itself in our day to day lives during 2020. Cardify was using transactional data to identify trends in transaction spend. I was tasked with the management of the website, design, data analysis and content creation.

I worked alongside industry experts, my contributions helped in developing the operations of Cardify. As well as creating and managing different channels for our content. As of 2023, Cardify has been rolled up into the business and placed under Drop for Business.

What was Cardify?

As a product to help businesses identify key trends in spend data, Cardify created reports on trending topics with both transactional and survey data. This was essentially seen as the thought-leadership arm of the business. To facilitate this reporting, I had build a content management system as well as developed new designs to fit the brand theme.

When building the home page, the key feature was providing a key look at each of the features available on the website. From indexed spend data to insights around nascent investment assets such as crypto or even insight into Peloton's recent performance. The home page provides a sneak peek into the data itself as the data is hidden behind a soft paywall built with JavaScript to grow the subscriber base.

Furthermore to drive lead generation, product pages were built for each of the data arms of the parent business Drop. These highlight each of the features clients could receive as the articles written for Cardify can be translated to highly relevant data insights.

Product Process & Creating Articles

Cardify was created from Drop (its parent company), Drop provided credit and debit card data acquired with permission from users for loyalty rewards. Cardify had the access to the data of over 3M millenials and saw a GMV spend of over $40 billion dollars throughout my time. With this data we would then craft a report, based on the specifics of the story or a business request.

If we're looking at an example of a company, for example, Burger King would like to learn more about its customers. We would be able to compare a cross-section of the wallet for the business. By using our dataset to evaluate a individuals average spend, transaction frequency, as well as competitor spend. This being a key business value, as we could provide a full-view of consumer spend as well as distinctly ask those specific individuals a survey regarding the company itself. When executed at scale, providing the transactional data trend as well as quantitative insights is very powerful.

Make Small Process Improvements

As I managed the content creation for web channels, I was fluent with the languages used and tools required to create content. However, as the team grew some of the challenges were to lower the required design/technical fluency. Some of the challenges here were to lower the amount of technical input required as well as identify low fidelity ways to output content.

To faciliate this process and lower overall effort required I created automations and templates with Figma, SQL and Excel. This lowered the technical barrier to entry for team members to begin working on content, I developed design systems to replicate our Chart.js content with Excel and Figma. As well as lower the amount of manual work required for recurring reports such as the weekly spend tracker. This lowered the lead time required for a slight trade-off in interactivity, allowing us to cut the average time required for a complete report by more than a week versus the initial time frame. This allowed the business to provide more timely reports on topics which fulfils the initial product goal of this project/business.

See the Pen My Pen by User (@ibawad) on CodePen.

Another optimization I created specifically, was automating the weekly index reports. This was a low code solution using Figma plugins and a CRON job for Google exports. For example, the usage of the Google Sheets plugin and convenient frame naming can allow you to directly input content.

This provided a very quick solution, as you can create automated content filling with just a Google Sheet output & Figma. This can be easily done with a Google Sheets sync for low cost, letting you automate some areas easily open for human error. Content Reel is another option that also allows you to quickly do this in Figma.
